Data Deletion Instructions

Last updated: May 20, 2026

The short version: The Deletutu iOS app stores no personal data, so there is nothing for us to delete on the app side. The only place we may hold information about you is the marketing website waitlist (deletutu.com) — and we delete waitlist records on request, free of charge, within a few business days. To request deletion, email tututech.ltd@gmail.com.

1. What data Deletutu may hold about you

The data we may have falls into exactly two buckets — and we tell you in plain words how to remove each one.

  • iOS app data — nothing on our end. The app runs entirely on your device. We never receive your photos, your videos, your device identifiers, your IP address, your contacts, or any other data from the app. See our Privacy Policy for the full explanation.
  • Waitlist on deletutu.com — if you signed up on our marketing site, we store the name, email, and optional phone number you submitted, hosted on Supabase (EU region). That's the only identifiable data we keep.

2. How to request deletion

Send us a short email from the address you used to sign up so we can match the record quickly. No special form, no account, no friction:

Email: tututech.ltd@gmail.com
Subject line: Delete my data
Body: Optional — just say "please delete my data." If you signed up with a phone or a name different from the email account, mention that so we can find the right row.

Within a few business days we will:

  1. Locate any waitlist record matching your email (or other identifier you provide).
  2. Permanently delete it from our database. Supabase backups are rotated and the record is gone from backups within 30 days.
  3. Reply to your email confirming the deletion was completed.

If we find no matching record — which is the common case for App-Store-only users — we still reply to confirm that nothing was stored.

3. Deleting photos inside the iOS app

When you delete a photo with Deletutu, the photo is moved to the iOS Photos "Recently Deleted" album, where iOS keeps it for 30 days before permanent deletion. That's a system behavior controlled by Apple — Deletutu does not bypass it. You can recover or permanently delete those photos earlier from Photos → Albums → Recently Deleted on your iPhone.

Because Deletutu never uploaded the photos in the first place, there is nothing on our end to delete from this action.

4. Removing the app entirely

To remove Deletutu and revoke any permissions:

  1. Long-press the Deletutu app icon on your iPhone.
  2. Tap Remove App → Delete App.
  3. Optional: open Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos and confirm Deletutu is no longer listed.

Deletutu stores no preferences, accounts, or photo data outside the device. Removing the app removes everything related to it.

If you arrived here from a Facebook or Instagram review of our Meta Developer App (Deletutu, App ID 1652669642724968), the answer is the same: the app does not log in with Facebook, does not read your Facebook profile, and does not store any data from Meta on our side. Email tututech.ltd@gmail.com and we will confirm in writing that no record exists for your account.
